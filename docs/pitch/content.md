## Slide 3: Solution

### Financial stakes + peer audit. Loss aversion makes failure expensive.

**λ = 1.955** — Losing $39 feels like losing $76. That's the behavioral physics that drives Styx.

**Three pillars:**
1. **Financial escrow** — Users stake real money (Stripe FBO). Success = money back. Failure = money gone.
2. **Peer audit** — A trained auditor (a "Fury") verifies compliance. No self-reporting.
3. **Behavioral physics** — Loss aversion coefficient λ=1.955 means the pain of losing is ~2x the pleasure of gaining. Styx harnesses this asymmetry.

*Speaker notes:*
This is the core value proposition. λ=1.955 is from Tversky & Kahneman's prospect theory — it's the empirically measured ratio of loss sensitivity to gain sensitivity. Don't just say "loss aversion" — say the number. It makes the science tangible and differentiates Styx from vague behavioral claims.

*Key talking points:*
