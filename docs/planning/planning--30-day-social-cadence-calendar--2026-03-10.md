# 30-Day Social Cadence Calendar

This is the execution calendar for the two public channels:

- `Jessica` = discovery, trust, emotional specificity
- `Styx` = product proof, trust reinforcement, beta conversion

It assumes the current Phase 1 wedge remains:

- `No-Contact recovery`
- `iOS` private beta
- `Test-Money Pilot`
- `US allowlist`

## Operating Rules

1. Jessica is the human front door.
2. Styx is the credible destination.
3. Every CTA should route to one owned destination:
   `private beta waitlist`, `no-contact guide`, or `Do Not Text Your Ex Tonight`.
4. Do not force daily direct promotion.
5. Do not lead with `blockchain`, `Fury`, `wager`, or `real money`.
6. If a post performs unusually well, spin out follow-up posts within 48 hours.

## Post-Type Library

Use these repeatedly rather than inventing from zero.

### Jessica Post Types

1. `Pain Name`
   Say exactly what the audience is feeling.
2. `Pattern Interrupt`
   Challenge a common self-deception.
3. `Tactical Relief`
   Give one thing to do in the next 10 minutes.
4. `Founder / Operator`
   Explain what you are seeing while building.
5. `Invitation`
   Ask the right people into the beta.
6. `Conversation`
   Ask the audience to name a trigger, objection, or behavior.

### Styx Post Types

1. `What Styx Is`
2. `What the Beta Is / Is Not`
3. `How It Works`
4. `Scope / Safety / Trust`
5. `Product Proof`
6. `User Motion / Milestone`
7. `Apply / Waitlist`

## Story / Light-Content Rules

Jessica stories should:

- feel immediate
- ask simple questions
- respond to audience language
- show founder humanity

Styx stories should:

- reduce risk
- clarify product boundaries
- reinforce the waitlist path
- show quiet proof of life

## Platform Notes

Use the same message across platforms, but adapt format:

- `Instagram / TikTok`: hooks, face-to-camera, urgency, story sequences
- `Substack / email`: essays, reflections, authority, trust
- `X / Threads`: short sharp pattern interrupts, beta updates, quote posts

The message should stay stable even if format changes.

## Escalation Rules

Shift the next 7 days of content if:

- one post gets unusually strong saves/shares/comments
- one objection appears 3+ times
- one CTA clearly outperforms the others
- one audience segment responds disproportionately well

Do not shift just because of vanity impressions.

## Minimum Proof To Collect

Every week collect:

- top-performing post by saves / replies / meaningful comments
- top objection
- top CTA performer
- top source of qualified waitlist signups
- one phrasing users naturally use that you should reuse

## Bottom Line

This calendar exists to stop the two channels from blurring together.

Jessica should feel like the trusted human guide.
Styx should feel like the credible product.
Both should route into the same owned funnel.
