# State of the Union (March 4, 2026)

## Purpose
This is the plain-language investor snapshot of where Styx stands on the Alpha-to-Omega path.

## One-Line Status
Styx has moved from concept into a real, testable product foundation, and is now in the risk-reduction stage before scaled real-money rollout.

## Proof of Work Completed
- We completed full document digestion across research, planning, legal, and brainstorm sources.
- We processed **52/52 files**, covering **711,300 words** and **7,616 extracted elements**.
- We converted unresolved high-priority gaps into an executable delivery queue.
- We mapped **24 unresolved P0/P1 features** into **19 implementation tickets** with ownership-ready scope.

## Where We Are on the Alpha→Omega Path
| Phase | Current Status | What This Means in Business Terms |
|---|---|---|
| Phase Zero: Foundation Story | Complete | Positioning, thesis, and product frame are coherent and defensible. |
| Phase Alpha: Core Trust Layer | Mostly complete | Core ledger, evidence trail, and baseline product mechanics are in place. |
| Phase Beta: Regulatory Shield | In progress | Safety/compliance controls exist, but some critical gates remain before broad money movement. |
| Phase Gamma: Proof Integrity Network | In progress | Anti-fraud and review systems are active, but proof integrity still needs deeper hardening. |

## What Is Already Real Today
- A working no-contact accountability flow with daily check-ins and consequence logic.
- Cohort and pod accountability structures with active/out visibility.
- Initial pricing model enforcement for the MVP structure.
- Compliance-facing legal surfaces live in product (rules, responsible use, policy links).
- Operational release discipline via readiness contracts and repeatable go/no-go checks.
- Early B2B foundations (CRM connector path and consumption billing primitives).

## What Still Blocks Full-Scale Rollout
- Real-money settlement execution and reconciliation reliability.
- Native camera-first proof capture for stronger submission integrity.
- KYC runtime enforcement and tiered stake limits.
- Fail-closed jurisdiction policy for unresolved location confidence.
- Refund-only legal contingency engine by jurisdiction.

|---|---|---|

| Payment rails for sensitive category | Medium-High | Settlement abstraction and provider-routing execution are defined as first P0 lane. |
| Verification trust attacks | Medium | Camera integrity, proof-processing completion, and redaction enforcement are now sequenced. |

| Enterprise readiness gap | Medium | Legal whitepaper gate + dashboard/leaderboard completion + compliance artifact controls are defined. |
