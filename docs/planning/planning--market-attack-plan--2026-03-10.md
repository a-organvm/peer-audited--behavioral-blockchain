# Market Attack Plan

Reviewed against current repo scope and external competitor sites on `2026-03-10`.

## Ground Truth

Styx should not market itself as a generic habit app during Phase 1.

The current product reality is:

- `iOS` external beta
- `No-Contact recovery` is the primary user journey
- `Test-Money Pilot`
- `US allowlist`
- broader recovery, productivity, B2B, and real-money expansion are not the current public product

So the first audience is not:

- everyone who wants better habits
- everyone who wants to drink less
- everyone who likes gamification

The first audience is:

- people in active breakup / no-contact pain who are already looking for help

## What Competitors Are Actually Doing

### 1. Recovery apps win with emotional specificity

Current pattern:

- [I Am Sober](https://iamsober.com/) leads with daily pledges, milestone streaks, specialized communities, and resource content.
- [Reframe](https://www.tryreframeapp.com/) leads with `science, not stigma`, a private community, guided challenges, and free-trial referral pages.
- [No Contact App](https://nocontact.app/) leads with a single emotional problem:
  `stay away from your ex`, then uses AI support, guided exercises, and SEO-heavy breakup content.

Implication:

- category leaders are not selling “behavior change” in the abstract
- they are selling relief for a named emotional state

### 2. Accountability apps win with mechanism-first hooks

Current pattern:

- [Beeminder](https://www.beeminder.com/money) sells commitment contracts and escalating monetary pain.
- [stickK](https://www.stickk.com/) sells commitment contracts, referees, supporters, anti-charity, and now group/workplace campaigns.
- [Habitica](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/habitica-gamified-taskmanager/id994882113) sells gamification, guilds, challenges, and social motivation.

Implication:

- these products attract users who already understand the mechanic they want
- they are not winning by emotional healing language
- they are useful comps for mechanics, but weaker comps for the Phase 1 audience wedge

### 3. Wellness-money products win with calculators and winners

Current pattern:

- [HealthyWage](https://www.healthywage.com/how-it-works/) leads with prize calculators, public challenge formats, success stories, and employer programs.

Implication:

- money-forward products reduce friction by letting users “see the payoff” immediately
- they also lean hard on social proof, press, and winner narratives

### 4. The best growth loops are community, content, and referrals

Common pattern across current winners:

- community support or cohort identity
- educational content or SEO article libraries
- referral or invite pages
- clear app-store CTAs and credibility signals

Implication:

- growth is not coming from one magic channel
- it comes from a stack:
  search capture + community + referral + social proof

## Where Styx Is Different

Styx has a stronger core than most of these products in one very specific area:

- consequence-backed no-contact recovery with explicit accountability structure

What competitors usually offer:

- streaks
- journaling
- AI chat
- motivation
- community

What Styx can uniquely offer:

- an actual accountability contract
- a defined daily attestation path
- an accountability partner / referee structure
- a more serious consequence system than streak loss alone

That is the wedge.

Do not dilute it by leading with:

- `behavioral blockchain`
- seven oath categories
- generic productivity
- real-money winnings
- social-economy / Fury complexity

## The Audience Hierarchy

### Primary audience: active no-contact strugglers

These are people already searching for:

- no contact app
- breakup recovery app
- how to stop texting my ex
- how long no contact lasts
- how to stop checking an ex’s social media

What they want:

- immediate relief
- a plan for the next 24 hours
- proof they are making progress
- something stronger than willpower

### Secondary audience: trusted intermediaries

These are:

- therapists
- coaches
- breakup recovery creators
- sobriety / recovery operators with adjacent audiences

What they want:

- a structured tool they can recommend
- something that increases between-session follow-through
- something safer and more disciplined than generic social apps

### Tertiary audience: public accountability nerds

These are:

- Beeminder / stickK / Habitica style users
- quantified-self and productivity communities

What they want:

- consequence systems
- proof
- discipline

This audience matters later, but it is not the clearest Phase 1 wedge.

## Multi-Level Marketing Attack Plan

### Level 1: Demand Capture

Goal:

- catch people already looking for help

Assets to build:

1. one landing page focused only on `No-Contact recovery accountability`
2. one landing page focused on `Private Beta`
3. three to five high-intent articles:
   - `What to do instead of texting your ex`
   - `How to survive the first 30 days of no contact`
   - `How to stop checking your ex on social media`
   - `What to do if you broke no contact`
   - `Why willpower fails after a breakup`
4. App Store copy and screenshots aligned to the same wedge

Rule:

- every asset must point to one CTA:
  `Join the private beta waitlist`

### Level 2: Borrowed Audience

Goal:

- get in front of people who already hold the audience’s trust

Targets:

- breakup recovery creators on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Substack, and podcasts
- no-contact and heartbreak communities
- therapist and coach newsletters

Offer:

- private beta access
- founder walkthrough
- recovery accountability guide
- early creator / advisor status for select partners

Rule:

- do not pitch “money app”
- pitch:
  `private accountability system for no-contact recovery`

### Level 3: Intermediary Distribution

Goal:

- make therapists and coaches a distribution layer

Assets:

1. a one-page practitioner explainer
2. a short `between-session compliance` guide
3. a referral code / invite workflow
4. a tight email sequence for therapists and breakup coaches

Best use:

- boutique practitioners
- recovery-adjacent coaches
- breakup / attachment specialists

Rule:

- position this as accountability infrastructure, not mass consumer social tech

### Level 4: Community Loop

Goal:

- create audience pull from the users themselves

Mechanics:

1. allow waitlist users to refer one or two friends
2. create cohort-based beta invites rather than open admission
3. use milestone share cards:
   `7 days`, `14 days`, `30 days`
4. create a founder-led Discord or small community only if moderation is real and active

Rule:

- keep community intimate and curated during beta
- do not open a wide social layer before moderation is ready

### Level 5: Authority Layer

Goal:

- make Styx look credible enough to trust in a vulnerable moment

Assets:

1. founder essay:
   `Why no-contact recovery fails without accountability`
2. behavioral science explainer
3. legal / safety posture summary
4. selected expert interviews or guest posts

Rule:

- authority should reduce fear, not increase abstraction
- explain the science in plain English

## Unique Ways To Find The Audience

These are the highest-upside non-generic plays for Styx.

### 1. Own the “don’t text your ex” emergency moment

Build:

- a simple emergency page or tool:
  `Do Not Text Your Ex Tonight`

Purpose:

- capture people in a live urge state
- route them into the private beta or waitlist

### 2. Build a relapse-prevention content cluster

Most breakup apps market healing in general. Styx should own:

- relapse
- urges
- accountability
- daily proof
- social media checking

That is much closer to the actual product.

### 3. Target creators with audiences, not giant influencers

Best targets:

- breakup coaches
- attachment-style educators
- sober-curious / self-control creators
- therapy creators with disciplined audiences

Why:

- smaller audiences with stronger trust convert better for a vulnerable product

### 4. Use practitioners as trust bridges

A therapist, coach, or recovery guide saying:

- `Use this if you keep breaking no contact between sessions`

is more valuable than a generic ad.

### 5. Turn the waitlist into a qualification system

Ask applicants:

- breakup stage
- no-contact duration
- top trigger
- whether they want accountability partner involvement

Use this to:

- segment cohorts
- personalize onboarding
- find the most urgent use cases

### 6. Create one unmistakable phrase

You need one phrase people can remember and repeat.

Current best candidate:

- `No-contact recovery with consequences`

Alternatives:

- `Accountability for heartbreak`
- `The app that stops you from texting your ex`
- `A commitment contract for no contact`

Do not lead with:

- `behavioral blockchain`

That can stay as internal philosophy or secondary copy, not the top-of-funnel hook.

## What Not To Do Yet

Do not spend current effort on:

- broad paid acquisition
- generic habit-app SEO before the wedge is proven
- public Fury economy marketing
- real-money positioning
- B2B enterprise campaigns as the primary launch motion
- mass creator seeding without a clear wedge and onboarding path

## First 30-Day Execution Stack

### Week 1

1. lock final Phase 1 public positioning
2. build the `No-Contact recovery` landing page
3. build the private beta waitlist form
4. define the outreach list:
   creators, therapists, coaches, communities

### Week 2

1. publish the first three search-capture articles
2. ship founder message / manifesto for this wedge
3. create the practitioner explainer PDF
4. create the referral / invite workflow

### Week 3

1. begin direct outreach to micro-creators and practitioners
2. run founder-led demos for handpicked partners
3. start admitting the first small allowlisted cohort

### Week 4

1. collect testimonial language from beta users
2. create milestone share assets
3. double down on whichever source brings the best users, not the most users

## Metrics That Actually Matter

Do not optimize for vanity growth first.

Track:

- waitlist conversion by source
- activation into first contract
- day-7 retention
- day-30 no-contact completion
- partner / creator referral conversion
- percentage of accepted beta users who match the target wedge

## Bottom Line

The winning move is not:

- `market Styx as a habit app`

The winning move is:

- own the no-contact recovery wedge
- use content, creators, and practitioners to capture high-intent users
- run a curated private-beta funnel
- build trust through specificity, not abstraction
- expand into broader accountability and B2B only after this wedge proves itself
