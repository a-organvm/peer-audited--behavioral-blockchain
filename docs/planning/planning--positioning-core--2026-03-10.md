# Positioning Core

This is the proposed Phase 1 messaging core for Styx. It is anchored to the actual beta scope, not the long-term platform vision.

## Scope Constraint

Phase 1 public positioning should reflect:

- `iOS` private beta
- `No-Contact recovery`
- `Test-Money Pilot`
- `US allowlist`

Do not lead with:

- `behavioral blockchain`
- generic habit app language
- real-money winnings
- broad seven-category platform language

## Recommended UVP

`Styx helps people stay in no-contact recovery by turning intention into a real accountability contract.`

Why this works:

- customer-centered
- tied to the actual Phase 1 user
- describes the value without over-explaining the machinery

## Recommended USP

`Unlike breakup and habit apps that rely on streaks, journaling, or motivation alone, Styx uses consequence-backed accountability and daily verification to help users stop breaking no contact.`

Why this works:

- names the alternatives clearly
- states the differentiator directly
- matches the core wedge identified in market research

## Recommended Positioning Statement

`For people struggling to maintain no contact after a breakup, Styx is a private accountability app that turns recovery into a real commitment contract. Unlike apps built around streaks, chat, or generic self-help, Styx adds daily attestation, explicit accountability, and consequences strong enough to interrupt relapse behavior.`

## Recommended One-Line Pitch

`No-contact recovery with accountability.`

## Tagline Options

Primary recommendation:

- `No-contact recovery with accountability.`

Secondary options:

- `The app that helps you stop texting your ex.`
- `A commitment contract for no contact.`
- `Accountability for heartbreak.`

Use with caution:

- `The Blockchain of Truth`

Reason:

- strong as internal philosophy or later-stage brand mythology
- too abstract and potentially misleading for top-of-funnel Phase 1 positioning

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Immediate relief, not vague self-improvement

Lead with:

- the pain of breaking no contact
- the urge to reach out
- the need for stronger structure than willpower

### 2. Accountability, not shame

Lead with:

- structure
- commitment
- consequences
- support

Avoid:

- humiliation framing
- punishment-first copy

### 3. Specific mechanism, plain language

Say:

- commitment contract
- daily check-in
- accountability
- recovery structure

Avoid:

- academic jargon
- system architecture language

### 4. Private beta discipline

Lead with:

- private beta
- test-money
- curated access
- US allowlist

This keeps expectations honest and lowers trust friction.

## Audience-Specific Variants

### Consumer beta

`Styx helps you stay in no contact when willpower is not enough.`

### Practitioner / therapist / coach

`Styx gives clients a structured accountability layer for no-contact recovery between sessions.`

### Creator / audience partner

`Styx is a private accountability system for people who keep breaking no contact after a breakup.`

## What This Positioning Is Not

It is not:

- a generic productivity app
- a gambling app
- a public social challenge app
- a broad B2B mental health platform
- a real-money consumer finance product

## Approval Note

Per growth governance, any change to the positioning statement, tagline, or core messaging framework requires human approval from product leadership before broad rollout.
