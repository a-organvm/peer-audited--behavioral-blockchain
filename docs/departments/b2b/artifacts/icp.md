# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Overview

Styx's B2B model targets practitioners who assign behavioral contracts to their clients. The primary acquisition channel is direct practitioner partnerships — each partner brings a built-in client cohort, making practitioner sign-ups the highest-leverage revenue driver. This document defines the Ideal Customer Profile across three tiers, with qualifying and disqualifying criteria.

## Tier 1: Best Fit (Primary Target)

### Profile: Licensed Therapists Specializing in Addiction/Recovery

These practitioners have the strongest product-market fit because their clients face the highest behavioral stakes (relapse, no-contact violations, sobriety milestones) and benefit most from financial accountability between sessions.

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Licensure** | LCSW, LPC, LMFT, PsyD, PhD (clinical psychology) |
| **Specialization** | Addiction recovery, substance abuse, CBT, DBT, relationship counseling |
| **Active client load** | 5-20 clients currently in active treatment |
| **Practice size** | Solo practitioner or 2-5 person group practice |
| **Technology comfort** | Already uses digital tools with clients (telehealth, client portals, apps) |
| **Location** | US-based (initially), urban or suburban markets |
| **Revenue** | $100K-$500K annual practice revenue |
| **Current tools** | Uses at least one practice management tool (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane) |

**Why they buy:**
- Client follow-through between sessions is their #1 frustration
- They already believe in evidence-based interventions
- Financial stakes are a natural extension of behavioral activation techniques
- Recovery Oaths directly support no-contact and sobriety treatment plans
- They want measurable outcome data to demonstrate treatment efficacy

**Expected plan:** Solo ($49/mo) or Practice ($199/mo)
**Average client contracts per month:** 5-15
**Expected monthly platform revenue per partner:** $49-$199 (subscription) + $45-$135 (platform fees from client contracts)

### Profile: Certified Executive/Life Coaches

Coaches operate in a less regulated space and adopt tools faster. Their clients are typically high-income professionals who respond strongly to financial incentives.

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Certification** | ICF (ACC, PCC, MCC), CCE, or equivalent |
| **Specialization** | Executive coaching, leadership development, career transition, performance coaching |
| **Active client load** | 5-25 clients |
| **Pricing model** | Premium ($200-$500/session) — clients can afford Styx stakes |
| **Technology comfort** | High; uses CRM, scheduling tools, digital assessments |
| **Location** | US-based, metro markets |
| **Revenue** | $75K-$300K annual coaching revenue |

**Why they buy:**
- Accountability is literally their product — Styx automates the between-session component
- Their clients are affluent and motivated, making financial stakes highly effective
- Professional and Cognitive Oath categories align with coaching goals (project milestones, skill development, networking targets)
- Integrity Score provides quantifiable client progress data for coaching reviews

**Expected plan:** Solo ($49/mo) or Practice ($199/mo)
**Average client contracts per month:** 8-20

## Tier 2: Good Fit (Secondary Target)

### Profile: Fitness/Health Coaches with Premium Clientele

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Certification** | NASM, ACE, ISSA, Precision Nutrition, or equivalent |
| **Client type** | Premium personal training ($100+/session), wellness coaching |
| **Active client load** | 10-30 clients |
| **Technology comfort** | Uses fitness tracking apps, client portals |
| **Business model** | Independent or boutique gym/studio |

**Why they buy:**
- Biological Oath category (exercise, nutrition, hydration) maps directly to their service
- Clients already pay premium rates — adding a $39-$100 contract is marginal
- Completion rate data strengthens their marketing ("85% of my clients hit their targets")
- Financial stakes solve the "paid for sessions but doesn't do homework" problem

**Fit considerations:** Fitness coaches may need education on behavioral economics framing. Some may resist the "money at risk" model if they see it as punitive rather than motivational.

### Profile: Outpatient Rehab Centers

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Type** | Outpatient substance abuse treatment, IOP programs |
| **Size** | 10-100 active patients, 3-15 clinical staff |
| **Approach** | Evidence-based (CBT, motivational interviewing, 12-step facilitation) |
| **Technology** | Has an EHR system, comfortable with digital health tools |
| **Revenue** | $500K-$5M annual |

**Why they buy:**
- Recovery Oaths support post-discharge accountability (the highest-risk period)
- Financial stakes add a novel compliance mechanism to existing treatment protocols
- Multi-practitioner dashboard lets clinical directors monitor across their team
- Outcome data (completion rates, Integrity Scores) supports accreditation and insurance reporting

**Expected plan:** Practice ($199/mo) or Enterprise ($999+/mo)
**Sales cycle:** 30-60 days (Practice), 60-90 days (Enterprise — requires clinical director and compliance review)

## Tier 3: Expansion Target (Future)

### Profile: Corporate Wellness Programs

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Company size** | 50-500 employees |
| **Industry** | Tech, professional services, finance (high-stress, desk-bound) |
| **Existing wellness** | Has a wellness budget or EAP but struggles with engagement |
| **Decision maker** | HR director, wellness program manager, or Chief People Officer |

**Why they buy (eventually):**
- Employee wellness ROI is hard to prove — Styx provides completion rate data
- Voluntary participation model avoids coercion concerns
- Biological and Professional Oath categories align with corporate wellness goals
- Enterprise tier (SSO, data lake, webhooks) integrates with existing HR systems

**Timing:** Post-launch (6+ months). Requires HIPAA BAA, SSO, and proven consumer traction.

### Profile: University Counseling Centers

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Type** | 4-year university, 5,000+ students |
| **Counseling staff** | 5-20 counselors |
| **Challenge** | High demand, limited capacity, poor between-session follow-through |

**Why they buy (eventually):**
- Students respond well to financial incentives (tight budgets make $39 meaningful)
- Academic Cognitive Oaths (study hours, attendance, assignment completion) fit student needs
- Counseling centers can extend their impact beyond the 1-hour session
- Integrity Score could integrate with student success platforms

**Timing:** Post-launch (12+ months). Requires institutional procurement process, potentially FERPA considerations.

### Profile: Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs)

| Attribute | Ideal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Type** | Third-party EAP provider (ComPsych, Lyra Health, Spring Health) |
| **Coverage** | 10,000+ covered lives |

**Why they buy (eventually):**
- Accountability tools improve utilization of EAP services
- Styx data demonstrates measurable behavioral change (outcome-based EAP reporting)
- White-label potential for large EAP providers

**Timing:** Post-launch (18+ months). Requires enterprise partnerships, white-label capability, and demonstrated efficacy data.

## Firmographic Qualifiers

| **Client interaction model** | Regular recurring sessions (weekly or bi-weekly minimum) |

## Psychographic Qualifiers

| Signal | Indicates Good Fit |
|--------|-------------------|
| "My clients know what to do, they just don't do it" | Accountability gap awareness |
| "I wish I could see what happens between sessions" | Between-session visibility need |
| Uses apps like Calm, Headspace, or Noom with clients | Comfort with digital behavioral tools |
| Publishes content about evidence-based approaches | Values empirical methods |
| Frustrated with self-reported progress journals | Recognizes self-reporting limitations |
| Tracks client outcomes quantitatively | Data-driven practice style |
| Active on LinkedIn or professional forums | Digital presence, reachable via content marketing |

## Disqualifying Signals

| Signal | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| **No digital presence** | Cannot be reached through content marketing; unlikely to adopt new software |
| **Serves minors (under 18)** | Styx is 18+ only — legal and ethical constraints on financial stakes for minors |
| **Liability-averse / compliance-heavy** | Practices that won't adopt any tool without 6+ months of legal review are not worth the sales cycle at current stage |
| **Anti-fintech stance** | Philosophical objection to financial incentives in therapeutic contexts — unpersuadable |
| **Cash-only billing** | Indicates low technology adoption; clients unlikely to have digital payment methods |
| **Religious counseling (non-licensed)** | Unregulated practitioners who may misuse accountability tools; liability risk |
| **Practices focused on children/adolescents** | Styx is 18+; no current plans for minor-appropriate version |
| **International (non-US)** | Payment, compliance, and support infrastructure not yet available outside US |
